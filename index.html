The Mission

Building for the Nation

India Innovates 2026 is a national-level civic tech hackathon focused on Applied AI, organized by HN and the Municipal Corporation of Delhi (MCD). It invites India's brightest student innovators to build working products, prototypes, or breakthrough technology ideas capable of transforming governance, urban systems, democracy, cyber security, and national infrastructure.

Under the banner of "Where Code Meets Constitution", selected teams showcase directly to investors, government ministries, bureaucrats, diplomats, founders, and ecosystem leaders at the iconic Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi. This is NOT a casual hackathon - it is a policy + technology national innovation platform.

This is positioned as a movement aligned with the vision of Viksit Bharat 2047, every line of code written here serves the Constitution's promise of justice, equality, and fraternity.

What Sets This Apart

This is NOT a build-on-site hackathon. Development must be completed BEFORE 28 March 2026. The finale day is exclusively for live demonstrations and on-the-spot judging by experts.

Top teams receive FREE exhibition booth space at Bharat Mandapam where they present to investors, government ministries, and startup founders. Outcomes include government + political party pitch opportunities, paid government apprenticeships, and potential full-time role conversions.

Problem Statements

4 Domains That Matter

Each team must select exactly ONE domain. Build solutions with real-world impact for governance, urban systems, democracy, and national infrastructure.

01

Urban Solutions

City intelligence, infrastructure, pollution, traffic & waste management.

  • Urban Flooding & Hydrology Engine - GIS-integrated predictive system for 2500+ micro flood hotspots
  • Hyper-Local AQI & Pollution Dashboard - Ward-wise real-time air quality ML system
  • AI Circular Waste Intelligence - Computer vision + IoT waste classification & route optimization
  • Dynamic AI Traffic Flow + Emergency Corridor - Real-time signal adjustment & green corridors
02

Digital Democracy

Governance systems, political tech, citizen services & democratic innovation.

  • Global Ontology Intelligence Graph & AI Booth Management System
  • Hyper-local geo-fenced civic notification & secure blockchain E-voting
  • AI Avatar platforms & multilingual AI calling agents
  • Public Service CRM, AI Co-Pilot for leaders & sentiment analysis dashboards
03

Cyber Security

Build systems for national-grade digital defense and cyber resilience.

  • Threat detection & real-time monitoring systems
  • Secure access & advanced encryption frameworks
  • Phishing prevention & anti-social engineering tools
  • Incident response & automated remediation platforms
04

Open Innovation

Your wildcard - any original tech solution for real-world problems.

  • Healthcare, Robotics, Agriculture, FinTech, DeepTech
  • Blockchain, AI/ML, Sustainability, EdTech
  • Smart Governance & any real-world problem-solving technology
  • Any original solution contributing to India's development goals

Rules

Guidelines

Eligibility & Teams

  • Open to all students across India
  • Team size: strictly 3 to 6 members
  • Solo participation is NOT allowed
  • Must select exactly ONE domain
  • Each participant can be part of only one team

Submission Rules

  • Submit PPT or PDF explaining idea, approach & impact
  • File format: TeamName_DomainName.ppt/.pdf
  • Non-compliant filenames may result in rejection
  • Project must be original, built specifically for this event
  • Open-source tools allowed; previously published projects discouraged
  • Download PPT Template

Finale Day Rules

  • Development must be COMPLETED before 28 March 2026
  • No coding or development time on finale day
  • Finale is demo + judging ONLY
  • Travel & accommodation are participant responsibility
  • Professional conduct is mandatory at all times

Important Notes

  • Plagiarism = immediate disqualification
  • Jury decisions are final and binding
  • Organizers may modify rules in exceptional cases
  • PPT quality determines physical access to finale
  • Only shortlisted teams receive exhibition booths
